Project Summary

This project develops a stochastic SEIR-type transmission model to quantify norovirus spread in restaurant environments. Using outbreak-size data from the CDC's National Outbreak Reporting System (NORS), the model is calibrated through cross-validation and full-grid parameter search.

We evaluate two practical mitigation strategies used in retail food service: (i) excluding symptomatic food workers and (ii) improving hygiene practices among staff. Sixteen policy scenarios spanning both intervention types and compliance levels are simulated to measure policy effects on outbreak size distributions and extreme-event risk.

Key Results

The analysis shows that improved hygiene and illness exclusion both reduce outbreak size, with combined interventions offering the strongest mitigation effect. Strict hygiene with high compliance produces the highest reduction in mean outbreak size, while combined strategies most effectively reduce the probability of large outbreaks (>100 cases).
